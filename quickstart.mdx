---
title: Quickstart
description: Upload a photo and generate your first string art pin sequence in minutes.
---

The String Art Generator runs entirely in your browser. No installation, no account, and no server required. Follow these steps to go from a photo to a complete pin sequence.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the generator">
    Navigate to [halfmonty.github.io/StringArtGenerator](https://halfmonty.github.io/StringArtGenerator/) in a modern desktop browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge recommended).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for OpenCV to load">
    The page loads OpenCV.js — a large WebAssembly library — from a CDN. The status bar at the top reads **"Please wait: loading..."** during this time.

    Wait until the status changes to **"Generator is ready."** before proceeding. This typically takes 5–30 seconds depending on your connection speed.

    <Warning>
      Do not upload an image until you see "Generator is ready." — uploads made before OpenCV finishes loading will not process correctly.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Adjust parameters (optional)">
    Three parameters control the output. The defaults work well for most photos, but you can adjust them before uploading:

    | Parameter | Default | What it controls |
    |-----------|---------|-----------------|
    | Number of Pins | 288 | How many nail positions are placed around the hoop |
    | Number of Lines | 4000 | Maximum thread passes drawn |
    | Line Weight | 20 | How dark each line appears in the error calculation |

    <Tip>
      Leave these at defaults for your first run. See [Configuration](/reference/configuration) for a full explanation of each parameter.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload your photo">
    Click **"Click Here and select an Image to Start"** and choose a photo from your device.

    <Tip>
      For best results, use a high-contrast close-up portrait or bold graphic. Avoid busy backgrounds or images with many fine details — these reduce definition in the final output.
    </Tip>

    The generator immediately begins processing. You will see your image appear cropped to a square and converted to grayscale.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Watch the generation progress">
    Generation runs through three automatic stages:

    1. **Calculating pins** — Pin coordinates are placed around the circle.
    2. **Precalculating lines** — All valid line paths between pin pairs are computed.
    3. **Drawing lines** — The greedy algorithm selects and draws lines one by one.

    During stage 3, a counter shows your progress: `X Lines drawn | Y% complete`. The canvas updates every 10 lines so you can watch the image emerge in real time.

    <Info>
      Generation takes roughly 30 seconds to a few minutes depending on your device. Keep the tab in the foreground — background tabs may slow down significantly due to browser throttling.
    </Info>

    <Warning>
      If the page becomes unresponsive, just refresh. If you already had a pin sequence saved, you can paste it back using the **"Already have steps?"** button — no need to regenerate.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and copy your pin sequence">
    When generation finishes, the status bar shows **"Complete"** and a large text area appears below the canvas containing your pin sequence — a comma-separated list of pin numbers like:

    ```
    0,127,22,147,276,144,21,126,20,142,9,139,270,140,...
    ```

    **Copy this entire sequence now and save it somewhere safe** (a text file, notes app, etc.). This sequence is your output — if you close the page, you will need to re-run the generator to get it back unless you saved it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your next step">
    Two buttons appear below the pin sequence textarea:

    - **Start Creating** — Opens the step-by-step creation assistant. Each thread path is shown one at a time (highlighted in red), with keyboard controls to advance or go back. Use this mode when you are physically building the piece.
    - **Just Draw** — Replays the entire sequence on the canvas automatically at full speed. Use this to see a digital preview of the final result.

        Parameter tuning and best practices for better results.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Step>
</Steps>
