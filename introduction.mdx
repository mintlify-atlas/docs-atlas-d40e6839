String art is a craft technique where colored thread or string is wound between pins arranged on a board, creating geometric patterns and photorealistic images through the overlapping of many individual lines. The appeal is in its precision — images built entirely from straight lines, yet capable of producing smooth curves and tonal gradations.

The String Art Generator is a browser-based tool that automates the hardest part of the craft: figuring out which pins to connect and in what order. You upload a photo, the tool analyzes it, and outputs a numbered pin sequence. You then use that sequence to guide your physical build — wrapping string from pin to pin in order.

## How it works

The generator processes your image through four stages:

1. **Image preparation** — Your photo is cropped to a square, converted to grayscale, and circle-masked to match the round hoop.
2. **Pin layout** — Pin coordinates are calculated around the circumference of the circle (288 pins by default).
3. **Line precalculation** — All valid lines between pin pairs are computed and ranked by how much darkness they would add to the image.
4. **Greedy line selection** — The algorithm iteratively picks the line that best matches the remaining darkness in the image, up to the configured maximum (4000 lines by default).

The result is a comma-separated sequence of pin numbers. You copy that sequence and use it as a step-by-step guide to build the physical piece.

## Why use this tool

Calculating an optimal pin sequence by hand for hundreds of lines is not feasible. This tool does that computation in your browser — no installation, no account, no server-side processing. The entire algorithm runs locally using OpenCV (loaded from CDN) and standard browser APIs.

## Prerequisites

You need only a modern desktop browser with WebAssembly support. All major browsers released after 2017 qualify. No plugins, extensions, or local software are required.

OpenCV is loaded asynchronously from a CDN when the page opens. The status bar displays **"Generator is ready."** once loading is complete. Do not upload an image until you see that message.
